**Body**

But before we dive into the promises and pitfalls, let's take a moment to understand what quantum computing really is.

In traditional computing, a bit is the smallest unit of data and can represent either a 0 or a 1. Quantum computing, however, uses quantum bits, or qubits, which can represent both 0 and 1 at the same time, thanks to a property called superposition. On top of that, qubits can also interact with each other through entanglement, a phenomenon where the state of one qubit can instantly affect the state of another, no matter the distance.

Now, what does all this mean for AI research in 2024? According to Prof. Ahmed Banafa, quantum computing is one of the most promising areas for AI research.
